# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Independent creators and studio teams using a local-first desktop client to plan, assemble, and iterate on AI-driven video work. They need one workspace that keeps ideation, shot planning, assets, prompts, generation, and revision close together without depending on a remote web app for the primary workflow.

## Product Purpose
This product exists to turn AI video creation into a coherent local workflow. It helps users move from idea to script, storyboard, assets, model runs, and video outputs inside a single client so they can iterate quickly, keep control of local state and configuration, and treat generation as part of a directed production pipeline rather than a loose collection of tools.

## Brand Personality
Restrained, precise, trustworthy. The interface should feel calm and intentional, with the clarity and confidence of Apple-like product craft, while still reading as a serious creative tool for production work. The emotional goal is confidence during complex creation, not spectacle.

## Anti-references
Do not make this look like a flashy cyberpunk AI tool. Avoid noisy gradients, decorative futurism, aggressive neon theatrics, and visual effects that compete with the work. Do not make it feel like a messy node-experiment sandbox with inconsistent controls, unclear hierarchy, or chaotic panel behavior.

## Design Principles
1. Keep the tool subordinate to the workflow: the interface should reduce friction between planning, generation, and revision.
2. Prefer calm precision over spectacle: visual decisions should communicate control, reliability, and craft.
3. Make complex systems legible: dense workflows are acceptable when structure, hierarchy, and affordances stay clear.
4. Preserve local trust: configuration, assets, and generation state should feel inspectable, understandable, and under user control.
5. Support iterative production: every major surface should help users compare, branch, refine, and continue work without losing context.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast and interaction standards. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for non-essential animation. Do not rely on color alone to communicate state. Maintain clear focus states, keyboard-reachable primary actions, and readable dense layouts for long creative sessions.
